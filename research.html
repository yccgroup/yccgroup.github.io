Research

Theory and computation for molecular systems

We build models and algorithms to study molecular behavior, from electronic and quantum dynamics to biomolecular function and free-energy landscapes.

01

Statistical mechanics and partition functions

We develop estimators and computational strategies for molecular partition functions, thermodynamic quantities, and configurational integrals in systems large enough to be chemically meaningful.

02

Molecular simulation and free-energy methods

We use molecular dynamics and enhanced sampling to connect atomistic interactions with structure, dynamics, selectivity, and thermodynamic stability in molecular and biomolecular systems.

03

Quantum dynamics and nonadiabatic processes

We are interested in time-dependent molecular phenomena, electronic decay, vibronic coupling, and theoretical descriptions of molecular processes beyond simple Born–Oppenheimer pictures.

04

Biophysics and computational biology

We investigate the molecular basis of biological function using simulation, structure-based modeling, and quantitative analysis of biomolecular interactions.

05

Computer-aided molecular discovery

We explore computational approaches for molecular recognition, peptide and drug discovery, and machine-learning-assisted modeling when data and physical principles can be used together.

06

Scientific software and reproducibility

We value clear, reusable software and transparent workflows that allow theoretical ideas to become practical tools for the broader molecular science community.
